About Me

Mathematics has always been a gateway for my understanding of the world. Since my childhood, I always wondered about patterns behind complex systems. I soon found mathematics as my direction to pursue this curiosity.

Academic Achievements

Euclid Contest (Distinction), AMC-12 (Honor Roll), AIME (score: 12), and Physics Bowl (Silver).

These achievements propelled me to uncover more about the depths of mathematics. Through these, I developed a love for analysis, inspired by books like How to Think About Analysis by Lara Alcock and concepts such as the Mean Value Theorem.

Exploring Beyond Mathematics

In A-level Further Mathematics, I learned to build and solve differential equations, inspiring me to explore multivariable calculus, the Jacobian matrix, and gradient vectors. I applied these in research optimizing fire station placements in a city. Through this, I explored the momentum gradient descent method, showcasing the real-world application of mathematics.

Financial Mathematics

My curiosity led me to financial mathematics, inspired by the binomial tree model and the Black-Scholes formula. Exploring Brownian motion and Ito processes fascinated me, bridging mathematics with real-world phenomena like asset pricing.

Extracurricular Passions

Outside academics, I enjoy performing arts and music. My achievements include:

  • Regional first prize in a drama performance about the Long March
  • First prize in the IPEA percussion competition
  • Grade 10 certificate of drums

Music analysis has also deepened my understanding of measure theory, enriching my appreciation of frequency distributions and auditory experiences.

Looking Ahead

Mathematics is not just a subject for me; it is a mindset. It enables me to combine intuition with rigour, solve real-world problems, and explore fascinating models like those in finance. I look forward to pushing the frontiers of mathematical research alongside mentors and peers at university.
